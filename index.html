Solar System Journey

Explore the wonders of our cosmic neighborhood. The solar system consists of the Sun and the celestial objects bound to it by gravity, including planets.

Mercury

The smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun, Mercury is only slightly larger than Earth's Moon.

Diameter: 4,880 km
Orbit Period: 88 Earth days
Surface Temp: -173°C to 427°C

Venus

Similar in size to Earth, Venus spins slowly in the opposite direction from most planets with a toxic atmosphere.

Diameter: 12,104 km
Orbit Period: 225 Earth days
Surface Temp: 462°C

Earth

Our home planet is the only place we know of so far that's inhabited by living things, with vast oceans and a life-supporting atmosphere.

Diameter: 12,742 km
Orbit Period: 365.25 days
Surface Temp: -88°C to 58°C

Mars

The Red Planet is a dusty, cold desert world with a very thin atmosphere and seasons, polar ice caps, and more.

Diameter: 6,779 km
Orbit Period: 687 Earth days
Surface Temp: -153°C to 20°C

Jupiter

More than twice as massive as all other planets combined, this gas giant has a Great Red Spot storm larger than Earth.

Diameter: 139,820 km
Orbit Period: 12 Earth years
Surface Temp: -108°C

Saturn

Adorned with thousands of beautiful ringlets, Saturn is unique among the planets with its spectacular ring system.

Diameter: 116,460 km
Orbit Period: 29 Earth years
Surface Temp: -139°C

Uranus

Uranus rotates at a nearly 90-degree angle from the plane of its orbit, appearing to roll around the Sun like a ball.

Diameter: 50,724 km
Orbit Period: 84 Earth years
Surface Temp: -197°C

Neptune

Dark, cold, and whipped by supersonic winds, Neptune is the only planet in our solar system not visible to the naked eye.

Diameter: 49,244 km
Orbit Period: 165 Earth years
Surface Temp: -201°C

Moon

From lighting up our skies to preserving evidence of our solar system’s history, Earth’s closest neighbor plays a pivotal role in the study of our planet and beyond.

Diameter: 3 474,8 km
Orbit Period: 27 days
Surface Temp: -298°F to 260°F
